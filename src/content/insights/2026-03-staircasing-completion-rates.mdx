## The national picture

Drawing on anonymised transaction data from more than forty housing associations across England, this briefing maps staircasing completion rates over calendar year 2025. The headline finding is that completion rates vary by more than a factor of two between the strongest and weakest performing regions — a variance that is not explained by local market conditions alone.

Providers operating in the same sub-regional markets can post completion rates that differ by fifteen percentage points or more. The drivers are operational, not structural: lease data quality, valuation turnaround, and the speed of solicitor instruction each account for a measurable share of the variance.

## Where transactions stall

The data identifies three recurring failure points. First, lease clause ambiguity — particularly around staircasing premia and rent recalculation — triggers legal queries that can add eight to twelve weeks to a transaction. Second, outdated valuations force re-instruction when transactions drift past the three-month validity window. Third, provider-side document assembly (management pack, service charge accounts, EWS1 where applicable) remains the single longest queue in most portfolios.

Providers that have digitised lease data extraction and moved to on-demand management pack generation are completing staircasing transactions thirty-one percent faster, on average, than their peer group.

## Implications for 2026

Our projection for 2026 is that the operational gap widens. Providers with structured lease data, automated valuation instruction, and integrated conveyancing workflows will continue to pull ahead. Those relying on spreadsheet-based tracking and manual document assembly face compounding drag as transaction volumes recover.
